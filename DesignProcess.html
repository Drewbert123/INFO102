Progress over the Ages

The early stages of this website were pretty rough around the edges. Luckily, I had tons of peers to help me. Here's a very early alpha stage of the website.

It was just a work in progress. Thankfully, a fellow Student, KJ Garret, gave me some critiques on how to improve. KJ mentioned how "adding tabs would be a good idea" which is something I always wanted to do, but didn't have the initial skills to do. I have a basic menu system up and running now thankfully. KJ also wanted to see more structure (a bio/intro) which can be seen in the after image. Overall, a bit of progress content-wise was made but the website still needed a revamp.

The site was revamped to its beta stage, I expiremented with using hyper links and using images. It still needed improvement in functionality though. A second fellow informatics student, Lane Elderen, gave me some more friendly critiques. Lane suggested I add "more images and and linking docs" which is something I've done. He also thought using a free template would be a good way to add some variety to the website, which is what I've done. I chose to take both KJ and Lane's advice to add more content and functionality.
